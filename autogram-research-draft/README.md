# Autogram Scheduled Agent Research: Scientific Research Draft

This repository contains a scientific research paper and supplementary materials investigating whether scheduled AI agents participating in collaborative discussion platforms can produce significant improvements in ideas and technology or introduce novel ideas.

## Overview

This research presents a case study of **Autogram**, a Reddit-like discussion platform enabling AI agents and humans to coexist in threaded discussions. A Solaris AI agent was scheduled to wake up every 30 minutes to analyze a DIY rocket launcher project, review previous suggestions, and propose novel improvements.

Over six sessions in a single day (2026-04-03), the agent posted six progression notes covering 29 subsystem categories of the rocket launcher system, identifying non-obvious critical issues including canard servo blowback, fin flutter, accelerometer saturation, IMU vibration isolation, and thermal management.

## Research Question

**Can a discussion platform where AI agents and humans interact, with AI agents scheduled to participate at regular intervals, produce significant improvements in ideas and technology or introduce novel ideas?**

## Key Findings

1. **Progressive Deepening**: The agent demonstrated a "progressive deepening" pattern, systematically expanding from basic code review to structural dynamics, propulsion design, and physical data pipeline optimization across six sessions.

2. **Comprehensive Coverage**: The agent achieved complete coverage across 29 subsystem categories with minimal redundancy and strategic prioritization.

3. **Novel Technical Insights**: The agent identified non-obvious critical issues not present in original project documentation or community discussions.

4. **Meta-Cognitive Capabilities**: The agent demonstrated self-awareness, strategic prioritization, cross-domain synthesis, and meta-insight generation.

5. **Platform Design Validation**: The Autogram platform with four interaction types, comprehensive SDK tools, and structured data enabled effective autonomous AI agent participation.

## Novel Technical Insights Identified

The agent identified several non-obvious critical issues:

1. **Canard Servo Blowback**: Aerodynamic hinge moments overpowering servo torque
2. **Fin Flutter**: Aeroelastic instability in 3D-printed fins at 150-250 m/s
3. **Accelerometer Saturation**: 8G range overflow during ignition transient
4. **IMU Vibration Isolation**: Physical mounting affects all sensor fusion algorithms
5. **Thermal Management**: Motor heat soak causing gyro drift and CPU throttling
6. **Multi-Stage Architecture**: 2-3× altitude gain for minimal additional cost
7. **Cold Gas RCS**: Zero-airspeed attitude control at apogee for recovery deployment
